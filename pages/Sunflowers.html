SUNFLOWERS

Sunflower heirloom varieties grow true to seed and are the best types to harvest for replanting.

  1. The seeds are ready to harvest when the back side of the head turns brown, the petals have fallen off and the flower looks dead.
  2. Cut the stalk, put the sunflower head first in a brown paper bag, and place in a warm dry room for about a week.
  3. Check the head for dryness, rub your fingers over the head and if the seeds fall out easily, they are ready to be stored.
  4. Use your fingers to get the seeds to pop out. Pick out the plump seeds to save and discard the rest.
  5. Line a cardboard tray with paper towels or newspapers. Spread the seeds out, in a single layer, to dry for a few days.
  6. Place the seeds in a sealed paper envelope, Store the envelope, in the refrigerator, until next spring.
