DIANTHUS

Dianthus are grown as annuals and perennials depending on the type. I am focusing on the annual varieties here.

  1. If you have your Dianthus plants planted in the ground, in a more protected area, it may come back next year.
  2. In the fall, do not pull your well established plants up. Leave them where they are and keep them watered.
  3. If fallen leaves nestle in around the plants, let them be as they will provide shelter for the plants over the winter.
  4. If no leaves have fallen around the plants, either put some leaves or grass clippings around the plants.
  5. In the spring, when it start warming up, you will need to gently remove the leaves or grass clippings so they can get light to grow.
  6. Whether this works or not will depend on its environment. I have some more sheltered areas (trees around), of the yard, where they keep coming back. I have tried them in more open areas and they did not come back.
  7. You are not out anything, by leaving the plants, to see if they will grow again in the spring.
