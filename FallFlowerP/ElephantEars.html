ELEPHANT EARS

In zones 1-6, Elephant Ears are grown as annuals, from bulbs. With the proper steps, elephant ears can grow again year after year.

  1. In the fall, after the first frost, dig up the elephant ears. (If it is near the average first frost time in your region.
  2. Cut off the foliage, at the top of the bulb or tuber.
  3. Take off the excess soil. Leaving some soil attached is good.
  4. Elephant Ears multiply. Dividing the bulbs/tubers can be done now or when you replant them again. I like to wait and divide them later.
  5. Place the bulbs/tubers, in a single layer, on a cardboard tray or in a box. If you use a cardboard box, leave the box open.
  6. Place the cardboard tray or box, in the basement or in an attached garage. A cool storage location should not get below 45 degrees Fahrenheit.
  7. After a few weeks of drying out, transfer the bulbs/tubers into a closed cardboard box or a paper bag. You can add peat moss to the bulbs/tubers; however, it is not necessary.
